Cesar Chavez

"Si, Se Puede!"

Cesar Chavez was a Mexican American farm worker, who later became an American labor leader and civil rights activist.

Interesting Fact: The slogan "Si, Se Puede" was co-written by Cesar Chavez. Translated in English: "Yes, it is possible." or "Yes, you can." The slogan was later embraced by President Obama Obama during his bid for the democratic party nomination in 2008.

Timeline of Cesar Chavez Life

  • 1927 - March 31, Cesar Estrada born near Yuma, Arizona
  • 1942 - Chavez begins as a farmworker
  • 1944 - Chavez begins his military service in the US Navy, which last 2 years.
  • 1946 - Chavez joins the National Agricultural Workers Union, his first.
  • 1948 - Chavez and his family join the National Farm Workers Labor Union
  • 1958 - Chavez organizes strikes, marches, and a boycott of merchants in Oxnard to protest local unemployment.
  • 1962 - Chavez leaves The CSO and moves to Delano where he founds the Farm Workers Association
  • 1965 - The NFWA and Agricultural Workers Organizing Committee start the grape boycott.
  • 1966 - In March, Chavez marches with 75 others from Delano where he founds the Farm Workers Association
  • 1968 - In February, Chavez begins his historic 25 days fast.
  • 1969 - UFW declares National Grape Boycott Day
  • 1970 - In December, Chavez is imprisoned for challenging injunction against the boycott.
  • 1973 - UFW celebrates first convention in Fresno.
  • 1975 - Calfornia Supreme Court declares the short-handled hoe an Unsafe Hand Tool thus banned by California law.
  • 1977 - An Agreement was reached that gave the UFW the sole right to organize farm workers.
  • 1984 - Chavez announces a new grape boycott, this time focused on pesticides.
  • 1988 - Chavez fasts for 36 days to protest pesticide use.
  • 1993 - April 23, after a fast of several days, Chavez dies in his sleep of unknown cause.
  • 1994 - Chavez posthumously receives the US Medal of Freedom from President Clinton
  • 1996 - Made a memorial of his history
